Engineering Capstone Project

Flight Control System Optimisation for a Cessna 182

Role: Flight Dynamics & Simulation Engineer

This multidisciplinary aerospace engineering capstone investigated the development, optimisation and verification of a Cessna 182 longitudinal flight-control simulation using MATLAB and Simulink. My primary contribution was the development and implementation of the nonlinear flight-dynamics model, together with aircraft trim analysis, manual Genetic Algorithm integration, atmospheric disturbance modelling and interpretation of the resulting aircraft responses.

Engineering Challenge

The project required a simulation-driven method capable of evaluating flight-control parameters while preserving stable longitudinal aircraft behaviour across different operating conditions. The simulation also needed to demonstrate realistic response under atmospheric turbulence, provide repeatable trim conditions and generate sufficient evidence for verification of aircraft stability, control authority and propulsion behaviour.

My Engineering Responsibilities

The project was completed by a multidisciplinary engineering team. The responsibilities below describe the areas that I personally developed, implemented or directly supported.

Flight-Dynamics Development

Developed the nonlinear three-degree-of-freedom longitudinal flight-dynamics model representing aircraft airspeed, angle of attack, pitch attitude, pitch rate, altitude and range.

MATLAB and Simulink Implementation

Implemented the aircraft simulation environment in MATLAB and Simulink and developed custom MATLAB S-functions for calculation of the aircraft dynamic response.

Aircraft Trim Analysis

Established steady-state cruise conditions so that controller evaluation and disturbance testing began from physically consistent equilibrium points.

Manual Genetic Algorithm Integration

Integrated a manually implemented Genetic Algorithm workflow with the flight simulator to evaluate candidate parameter sets through repeated simulation and performance comparison.

Atmospheric Disturbance Modelling

Implemented Dryden gust disturbances to assess aircraft response under stochastic turbulence rather than ideal still-air conditions.

Verification and Interpretation

Analysed aircraft state, control-surface and propulsion responses and supported integration, verification and validation of the complete simulation framework.

Technical Approach

The project combined mathematical modelling, simulation, optimisation and verification within one structured aerospace engineering workflow.

1

Aircraft Mathematical Model

The aircraft was represented through nonlinear longitudinal equations of motion and aerodynamic relationships appropriate for the Cessna 182 cruise regime.

2

Simulation Architecture

The mathematical model was implemented through MATLAB S-functions and integrated within Simulink to enable repeatable simulation and state monitoring.

3

Trim Establishment

Trim values were identified before disturbance testing to ensure that thrust, drag, lift, weight and pitching moments were balanced at the initial condition.

4

Parameter Evaluation

The manual Genetic Algorithm workflow generated and compared candidate parameter combinations through successive flight-simulation runs.

5

Dryden Gust Testing

Stochastic gust inputs were introduced to assess whether the aircraft remained bounded and dynamically stable under mild atmospheric disturbances.

6

Verification

Aircraft responses were examined against stability, control-authority, cruise-performance and propulsion requirements.

Simulation Architecture

Simulink Flight-Dynamics Model

The nonlinear longitudinal aircraft model was implemented in Simulink using a custom MATLAB S-function. Control inputs were combined before entering the aircraft model, while the calculated aircraft states were separated into individual outputs for evaluation.

This architecture provided the simulation foundation for trim analysis, manual Genetic Algorithm parameter evaluation and Dryden gust-response testing.

Engineering skill demonstrated: translation of nonlinear flight-dynamics equations into a functional, modular MATLAB/Simulink simulation environment.
Short-Period Stability

Angle-of-Attack Response

The angle-of-attack signal remained bounded during the 100-second Dryden gust simulation, with variation remaining within approximately ±0.8 degrees of the nominal trim condition.

The absence of divergence or sustained bias indicated adequate aerodynamic damping and a dynamically stable short-period response under mild atmospheric turbulence.

Verification outcome: bounded angle-of-attack response with no divergent longitudinal behaviour.
Cruise Performance

Velocity Response

The aircraft maintained a cruise velocity close to the trim value of approximately 167.1 ft/s throughout the gust simulation.

The relatively small fluctuations demonstrated that the selected trim condition maintained an effective balance between thrust and drag and remained representative of steady cruise flight.

Verification outcome: near-constant cruise speed with no evidence of airspeed divergence.
Long-Period Stability

Altitude Response

The altitude response exhibited a gradual low-frequency excursion rather than a rapid or unstable departure from the initial 5000 ft condition.

This behaviour was consistent with a lightly damped phugoid response, where kinetic and potential energy exchange slowly while the aircraft remains dynamically stable.

Verification outcome: limited low-frequency altitude variation without rapid divergence.
Control Authority

Elevator Response

Elevator deflection remained within a narrow range of approximately ±0.3 degrees throughout the disturbance simulation, substantially below the available operational range of ±20 degrees.

The bounded response showed that aircraft pitch behaviour was maintained without excessive control-surface movement and indicated sufficient natural aerodynamic damping.

Verification outcome: stable elevator motion with significant remaining control authority.
Propulsion Behaviour

Throttle Response

The simulated engine output remained close to the selected cruise trim setting during both the velocity-sweep and elevator-sweep disturbance cases.

Only small fluctuations were observed during the 100-second simulations, demonstrating that the propulsion model maintained approximately constant power while the aircraft responded aerodynamically to atmospheric disturbances.

Verification outcome: stable cruise power with limited variation under stochastic gust excitation.

Engineering Decisions

Why a 3-DoF Longitudinal Model?

The project focused on pitch dynamics and longitudinal flight-control performance. A three-degree-of-freedom model provided sufficient fidelity for this objective while remaining computationally practical for repeated parameter evaluation.

Why MATLAB and Simulink?

MATLAB supported mathematical implementation, trim calculations and data analysis, while Simulink enabled the aircraft model and control inputs to be integrated within a repeatable dynamic simulation.

Why a Manual Genetic Algorithm?

The manually implemented workflow retained visibility over individual candidate solutions and enabled intermediate parameter combinations to be reviewed during development and verification.

Why Dryden Gust Modelling?

Dryden disturbances introduced statistically representative turbulence and allowed aircraft response to be tested under repeatable non-ideal atmospheric conditions.

Engineering Reflection

Developing the flight-dynamics and simulation environment reinforced the importance of accurate mathematical modelling as the foundation of flight-control system development. Integrating trim analysis, optimisation and atmospheric disturbance modelling also demonstrated how subsystem verification and iterative simulation contribute to reliable aerospace engineering outcomes. Working within a multidisciplinary team further strengthened my understanding of interface management, technical communication and the need to clearly distinguish individual subsystem responsibility from the wider project effort.
